Our Purpose

Why PERMA exists.

PERMA was founded on a quiet but powerful conviction: that no one should be left behind. For 50 years we have served Malaysians of every background — with urgency, dignity, and long-term commitment.

Our work is guided by the belief that dignity is not a privilege. It is a right.

Core Pillar One

Welfare

When hardship strikes, PERMA responds quickly and without condition. Our welfare programmes deliver immediate, practical support to those who need it most.

Food Aid 2024

PERMA distributed dry food worth RM17,000 to 100 underprivileged families in Dengkil as part of its bi-annual community project. PERMA committee members were present to hand over the packed groceries to the families. Lunch was served for all the 100 families present.

📊 Impact: 100 families | 🍱 Lunch served to all families | 💰 RM17,000

Food Aid 2025

PERMA extended support to Rumah Kebajikan Karunai Illam in Kepong by donating groceries worth RM1,001.15, along with a pressure cooker costing RM229.90. Lunch was provided for the children at a cost of RM520 bringing the total expenditure to RM1,751.05. Committee members Vick, Mangales, Vimala, Pushpa, Indira, Visahini and Sumithra were present during this visit.

The Home houses 20 girls who attend both Sekolah Kebangsaan and Tamil schools. Committee members also contributed personally by giving the children ang pows, spreading festive cheer and joy.

🏠 Rumah Kebajikan Karunai Illam | 👧 20 girls supported | 💰 RM1,751.05 total

Essential Equipment for Mary Orphanage

The Mary Orphanage, a well-run shelter for around 50 boys and girls, requested a replacement vegetable chiller. PERMA purchased a new 2-door chiller at a cost of RM2,450, delivered on 19 January 2024. A donor also kindly donated a rice cooker and blender. Committee members attended the handover ceremony.

Additionally, our Welfare Chairperson, Ms Vimala, secured a generous donor who contributed RM1,014 to cover dry provisions requested by the orphanage.

🏪 Equipment: Chiller System | 📅 19 January 2024 | 💰 RM3,464 total

COVID-19 MCO Emergency Response (11 Weeks)

During the COVID-19 MCO period, PERMA successfully completed 17 comprehensive projects within just 11 weeks to support families severely affected by the crisis. These initiatives provided timely assistance and relief, demonstrating PERMA's swift response and strong commitment to helping communities in times of crisis.

Crisis Response Video

Sri Lanka Flood Relief in collaboration with FOMSO

In response to the devastating floods caused by Cyclone Dithwah which resulted in over 600 deaths and widespread destruction, PERMA contributed RM5,000 towards FOMSO's humanitarian efforts.

FOMSO's team provided aid across several affected regions, including Kandy, Nuwara Eliya, Badulla, Mannar, Mullaitivu, and Vavuniya. Each area was given a budget of RM8,000. The FOMSO team that went to Sri Lanka for phase 1 of the relief efforts comprised of Mahen, Dr Geetha, Mr Surena and two other family members.

🌊 Cyclone Dithwah response | 🤝 In collaboration with FOMSO | 💰 PERMA contribution: RM5,000

02
Core Pillar Two

Health

PERMA believes prevention is as important as cure. Our health programmes focus on early detection, community education, and mental wellbeing.

Health Screening Programme

National Cancer Society Partnership

Mammogram screening for 27 ladies from underprivileged backgrounds was successfully carried out at the National Cancer Society. Transport was provided to NCS for all participants. Lunch was served for all ladies after the screening was completed. This important programme is carried out annually to ensure early detection and prevention.

🏥 Health Screening | 👥 27 women | 🚌 Transport Provided

PERMA supports emotional wellbeing through safe spaces, counselling access, peer support, and community-led awareness initiatives that reduce stigma and encourage early help-seeking.

These programmes translate medical knowledge into practical everyday habits, helping families make informed decisions around nutrition, hygiene, prevention, and long-term wellbeing.

PERMA’s maternal and child health work prioritises preventative care, early intervention, and family support so children can grow up healthier and caregivers can access the guidance they need.
