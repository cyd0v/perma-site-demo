Core Pillar One

Welfare

When hardship strikes, PERMA responds quickly and without condition. Our welfare programmes deliver immediate, practical support to those who need it most.

Food Aid 2025

PERMA supported Rumah Kebajikan Karunai Illam in Kepong, home to 20 girls from both national and Tamil schools, by providing essential groceries, kitchen equipment, and a shared meal for the children. Committee members also spent time with the girls and shared festive gifts, bringing additional joy and encouragement.

This initiative helped improve daily living conditions while ensuring better access to nutritious meals. More importantly, it created a warm and caring environment that uplifted the children's spirits and reinforced a sense of support and belonging.

🏠 Rumah Kebajikan Karunai Illam | 👧 20 girls supported

Food Aid 2024

PERMA carried out a food aid initiative in Dengkil, personally distributing essential groceries to 100 underprivileged families as part of its bi-annual community programme. Committee members were present to hand over the packed groceries and spend time with the families, fostering a genuine sense of care and connection.

This initiative provided immediate relief by helping ease the burden of daily food needs, allowing families to redirect their limited resources toward other essentials such as education and household expenses. Beyond material support, the effort reinforced a sense of inclusion and community — reminding recipients that they are seen, valued, and supported.

The gathering created a warm, welcoming environment where families shared a meal together, strengthening social bonds and lifting spirits. Overall, the programme contributed not only to food security but also to emotional well-being, leaving a meaningful and lasting impact on the community.

📊 100 families | 🍱 Shared community meal | 📍 Dengkil

Supporting Mary Orphanage, Setapak

Supporting the Mary Orphanage in Setapak, Kuala Lumpur has had a meaningful and lasting impact on the daily lives of nearly 50 children in their care.

Recognizing the orphanage's urgent need for reliable food storage, PERMA stepped in with funds to enable the purchase of a brand-new two-door vegetable chiller. This essential equipment now ensures that fresh produce can be safely stored for longer periods, improving food quality, reducing waste and supporting better nutrition for the children.

The initiative was further strengthened by the generosity of donors who contributed additional essential kitchen items including a rice cooker and a blender. These tools have enhanced the orphanage's ability to prepare nutritious, home-cooked meals efficiently for all residents.

In response to the orphanage's request for basic supplies, our Welfare Chairperson, Ms. Vimala, also secured a kind donor who contributed towards dry provisions. This contribution helped replenish essential food stocks, easing the financial burden on the orphanage and ensuring continuity in meal preparation.

Together, these efforts have significantly improved the orphanage's food management capabilities, creating a more stable and sustainable environment for the children. Beyond meeting immediate needs, this project reflects the power of community support in uplifting vulnerable lives and strengthening the foundation for their well-being.

🏻 Chiller, Rice Cooker & Blender | 👦👧 ~50 children | 📅 19 January 2024

COVID-19 MCO Emergency Response (11 Weeks)

During the COVID-19 MCO period, PERMA successfully completed 17 comprehensive projects within just 11 weeks to support families severely affected by the crisis. These initiatives provided timely assistance and relief, demonstrating PERMA's swift response and strong commitment to helping communities in times of crisis.

Sri Lanka Flood Relief in collaboration with FOMSO

In response to the devastating floods caused by Cyclone Dithwah which resulted in over 600 deaths and widespread destruction, PERMA contributed towards FOMSO's humanitarian efforts.

FOMSO's team provided aid across several affected regions, including Kandy, Nuwara Eliya, Badulla, Mannar, Mullaitivu, and Vavuniya. The FOMSO team that went to Sri Lanka for phase 1 of the relief efforts comprised of Mahen, Dr Geetha, Mr Surena and two other family members.

🌊 Cyclone Dithwah response | 🤝 In collaboration with FOMSO
PERMA Flood Relief Initiative in Chaah, Johor

In 2023, PERMA representatives Indira Somasundram and Sumithra V. Ananthan travelled to Chaah, Johor, to support communities affected by severe flooding. Their mission focused on delivering essential aid and helping restore a sense of stability to families who had been displaced or impacted by the disaster.

A total of 53 households received vital home essentials including crockery, cutlery, rice cookers, and gas stoves — items crucial for families working to rebuild their daily routines. Food supplies such as rice, cooking oil, cereals, instant noodles, and rice porridge were also distributed to ensure immediate nutritional needs were met.

Beyond material support, the initiative included visits to SJK Cantuman Chaah and SMK Chaah to assess flood damage and explore ways PERMA can contribute to the recovery of both schools, ensuring students can return to a safe and supportive learning environment.

This effort reflects PERMA's ongoing commitment to standing with communities in times of crisis — providing not only essential resources, but also hope and reassurance during the most challenging of times.

🏠 53 households | 🍚 Food & home essentials | 🏫 SJK & SMK Chaah visits

Core Pillar Two

Health

PERMA believes prevention is as important as cure. Our health programmes focus on early detection, community education, and mental wellbeing.

Mammogram Screening Programme

National Cancer Society Partnership

PERMA organised its annual mammogram screening programme in collaboration with the National Cancer Society, supporting 27 women from underprivileged backgrounds by providing access to essential screenings.

This initiative promotes early detection of breast cancer, helping improve health outcomes while removing barriers such as cost and accessibility. It also raises awareness on the importance of regular check-ups and encourages women to take a more proactive approach to their health.

Overall, the programme empowers women with greater confidence and access to care, making a meaningful impact in underserved communities.

🏥 Annual Programme | 👥 27 women | 🤝 National Cancer Society

Suara Minda — Pottery for Mental Wellness

Suara Minda (SM), supported by the Malaysian Mental Health Association and Selangor Mental Health Association, provides a supportive space for individuals on their mental health recovery journey.

Through pottery workshops, participants benefit from a calming and mindful activity that enhances focus, emotional expression and overall well-being. PERMA's support in providing organic clay for these sessions over a 6-month period enables these sessions to be carried out consistently, ensuring continuous engagement.

The initiative has a meaningful impact by building confidence, reducing stress and fostering a sense of purpose — helping individuals feel more empowered and supported in their road to recovery.

🏺 Pottery Workshops | 🤝 MMHA & SMHA | 📅 6-Month Support

These programmes translate medical knowledge into practical everyday habits, helping families make informed decisions around nutrition, hygiene, prevention, and long-term wellbeing.

Core Pillar Three

Capacity Building

Lasting change requires more than immediate relief. PERMA invests in education, skills, sports, and the arts — creating pathways beyond hardship.

Back-to-School Project

20 February 2025 · SJKT Ladang Kerling

PERMA visited SJKT Ladang Kerling where we were warmly welcomed by the Headmaster, Mr. Kalayvanan, Cikgu Sajim, the Penolong Kanan, and several other teachers. It was heartwarming to see the school maintained in excellent condition with 70 students currently enrolled, ranging from preschool to Standard 6.

Various activity books, exercise books, and other educational materials were distributed to the students. As a gesture of appreciation, the school presented a token of thanks to PERMA.

This effort has helped enhance classroom engagement, boost students' confidence and encourage a more positive approach to learning. By easing resource constraints, the project also supports teachers in delivering more effective lessons and ensures that every student has the tools needed to succeed.

Overall the initiative strengthens the school's ability to provide quality education while fostering motivation and a more inclusive learning experience.

🏫 SJKT Ladang Kerling | 👧 70 students | 📅 Feb 2025

Mentorship & Life Skills Programme at VTOC

2025

PERMA supports 31 VTOC students — primarily Indian and Orang Asli girls — through a holistic programme designed to build confidence, independence, and life skills. Through consistent monthly support, students are able to focus more fully on their vocational training without the added burden of financial stress, improving attendance, motivation and overall well-being.

To further strengthen personal development, PERMA introduced Karate classes, giving students the opportunity to learn self-defence while cultivating discipline, resilience, and self-confidence.

A highlight of the year was the orientation session held on 23 August 2025, led by Dr. Priya, with an energising Zumba session by Pushpa. PERMA's president, Ms. Indira, along with committee members Vimala, Pushpa and Mangales, reinforced a strong sense of community and support.

A key milestone was the launch of PERMA's personalised mentorship programme, pairing committee members with students to provide guidance, outings, and ongoing support — ensuring each student has a trusted mentor as they navigate personal and educational challenges.

Overall, the programme has created a nurturing environment where students feel supported, confident, and motivated to pursue their goals, equipping them with the skills and mindset needed for a brighter future.
